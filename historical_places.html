Historical Places


Red Fort

The Red Fort, also known as Lal Qila, is a historic fort in the city of Delhi in India. It was the main residence of the Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years, until 1857. It is located in the center of Delhi and houses a number of museums. The fort is considered to symbolize the power and prosperity of the Mughal Empire.


Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum located in Agra, India. It was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his third wife, Mumtaz Mahal. The Taj Mahal is widely recognized as "the jewel of Muslim art in India and one of the universally admired masterpieces of the world's heritage".


India Gate

The India Gate is a war memorial located in New Delhi, India, that was built in 1931 to commemorate the Indian soldiers who died in World War I and the Third Anglo-Afghan War. It is a prominent landmark in the city and stands as a symbol of national pride. The India Gate is also known as the All India War Memorial and is the site of the annual Republic Day parade.


Konark Sun Temple

Konark Sun Temple is a 13th-century CE Sun Temple at Konark in Odisha, India. It is believed to be built by king Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga Dynasty in 1255 CE. The temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most famous temples in India. The temple is shaped like a giant chariot, having elaborately carved stone wheels, pillars and walls.


Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar is a 73-meter tall tower located in Delhi, India. It is the tallest brick minaret in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The tower was built in the early 13th century by Qutb-ud-din Aibak, the first ruler of the Delhi Sultanate. The tower is made of red sandstone and marble and is decorated with intricate carvings and inscriptions. The tower has five distinct stories, each marked by a projecting balcony.


Hampi

Hampi is a village in northern Karnataka, India. It is located within the ruins of the city of Vijayanagara, the former capital of the Vijayanagara Empire. Hampi is a popular tourist destination known for its historical monuments, temples, and natural beauty. The ruins of the city are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
